1. Problem To Solve

Outcome for customer

They can make quick, fair, and easy decisions on who to hire, award opportunities to, or train based on people’s potential (aptitude & attitude) for cyber security, not just their skills or knowledge.

Why do they want to solve this problem?

There is a pipeline problem with cyber security and the field is growing/ever-changing. They need to be able to find staff from new sources and hire and train based on potential not just acquired skills, certifications or experience, as this quickly becomes outdated and exacerbates diversity issues in the field.

Why can’t they do it today?

It is not well-understood what good looks like and what it takes to make it in cyber security. It is believed that the only way to determine if someone is good is for them to try the field first, either through hobbies or education (expensive and privileged route). Companies copy each other’s job descriptions and are not strategic enough when linking the roles that they need to their company strategies. Consequently, many report a lack of entry level positions or job descriptions that ask too much from one person. Candidates also report unfair, biased and long interview/selection processes. This is partly due to techies being in charge of people and sometimes focusing too much on technical sides of cyber security and not the human side or having to do too much technical work at the top (due to a lack of staff). It is also because HR do not know how to hire cyber security people and delegate or gatekeep a lot; hiring managers and HR mistrust each other and there is a disjoint. There are no clear shared tools or understandings.

2. Target Audience

The rationale behind the choice of audience prioritisation is:

  1. Proximity/ease of reach: SANS is easiest to reach
  1. Competitive advantage: we can build a competitive moat with data we acquire from the academies for free
  1. Strongest resonance: the academies and existing SANS customers (of assessments) have the strongest resonance with our value proposition i.e. they already have faith in assessments

The perceived downsides of this approach is that they are predicted to financially worth less to us and could try to consume the product into their ecosystem before it’s fully fledged (wasting an opportunity for further growth).

3. Value Proposition

Our team is on a mission to build an industry leading tool that helps businesses enable people to unlock their cyber security potential by providing easy to use online assessments and deep insight, supported and validated by renowned psychometricians and industry experts.

  1. Scientifically validated; proven, trustworthy, repeatable
  1. Easy to use; a modern platform, designed with non-technical end users in mind
  1. Helps translate cyber security needs into people needs; breaks down silos between HR and hiring managers
  1. Fair; judges people who who they can be not what privileges or opportunities they’ve had
  1. Increases opportunities; finds hidden talent, increases team diversity and doesn’t rely on just one path in (as criminals don’t have just one path in)
  1. Backed by data; over time, data will become richer and insights into the cyber security workforce will grow

4. Competitive Advantage
Scale economics: medium-term strong

Over time, as more customers onboard, we will be able to deliver at scale, reducing overheads and making use of investments in new technology and content. This will offer us a strong competitive advantage over time

Network economics: long-term very strong

As more assessments are completed, we will have more data and will be able to develop more insights and derive more benefits.

Counter-positioning: short-term* very strong

*conflict with SANS? Alternative to certifications first approach. We are offering an alternative to train first and then see how they get on. We are offering an alternative to current cookie-cutter interviews with checkbox questions or highly biased ones.

Switching costs: long-term strong

Over time, people will embed us into their HR systems and will gather a wealth of historical data which they would lose if they were to switch.

Branding: short-term strong

We have a strong brand: good name, design etc and can attach ourselves to SANS’ prestige if we want.

Cornered resource: short-term strong

Using world-leading psychometricians and our contacts within SANS/GIAC to deliver content and a sales experience that nobody else has e.g. CTE or the Potential + training package.

Process power: no

We will not be leveraging any superior (internal) processes to enhance our value proposition.

Over competitors (in the immediate term- they could pivot at any time!):

  1. Generic technical skills testing

    Cyber security focused and based on aptitude, attitude and personality, not acquired skills/investment. Opening up to more people to quickly test their suitability for the field. Helping companies understand who is best suited for cyber roles, why and which roles.

  1. Generic psychometric testing Cyber security focused and based on aptitude, attitude and personality relevant to cyber security roles specifically. Opening up to more people to quickly test their suitability for the field. Helping companies understand who is best suited for cyber roles, why and which roles.
  1. WithYouWithMe.com Closest competitor. However we can compete on insights that are cyber security and built around companies’ use cases rather than the B2C market. Opening up to more people to quickly test their suitability for the field based on real opportunities.
  1. MyCyberPath.com A close competitor for RoleFinder assessment. However we can compete on insights that are cyber security and built around companies’ use cases rather than the B2C market. Opening up to more people to quickly test their suitability for the field based on real opportunities, not hypothetical.
  1. Traditional interviewing/performance reviews We will bridge communications gaps between HR & Hiring Managers. We will be able to provide a fast, fair and quick interview checkpoint. We will help companies understand the talent market and develop ways to make hiring and training decisions beyond existing processes, which can be biased and slow.
  1. ATS systems (adjacent) These systems filter out people based on keywords. This is not looking for people based on potential at all but companies have unfortunately adopted them for handling recruitment at scale.
  1. HRM systems (adjacent) These systems filter out people based on performance reviews and subjective reports. This can be biased and problematic. We should choose some to integrate with.

Levers/metrics that we need to monitor or experiment within growth phase to derive the best business model/pricing model:

New opportunities/month

We should measure how many new leads we get a month to ensure we have a healthy pipeline

Win rate/month

We should monitor how many of our leads we are able to convert to ensure that we are not blocking our own pipeline and tailor our marketing/sales materials/processes accordingly.

ACV (Average Contract Value) or ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)

We should monitor how much each of our customers, customer segments and customers in general are worth to us financially.

Assessments available per customer (not for affiliate)

We should monitor what content customers are buying and using to look for opportunities for different assessment-related revenue streams/packages.

Data Generated/Assessments taken per customer

We should keep an eye on the data that we are accruing as over time this will appreciate in value for us and help make our product more valuable.

(NRR) Net Revenue Retention/Churn rate quarterly

We need to monitor that we are keeping customers and that the customers that we are keeping are still paying as they were in the past.

Sales+Marketing:Sales Ratio/CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)

We need to keep a handle on the costs involved in winning new business.
